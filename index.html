1. Identify Hazards

A systematic walkthrough of the dental practice to spot anything that could cause injury or ill health.

Focus Areas

  • ✓ Clinical zones (sharps, chemicals, aerosols)
  • ✓ Common areas (trip hazards, electrical points)
  • ✓ Sterilization workflows and equipment

2. Assess Harm

Determine exactly who is at risk from the identified hazards and the nature of that potential harm.

Stakeholder Groups

  • ✓ Dental Nurses & Dentists (clinical risks)
  • ✓ Patients (cross-contamination, physical hazards)
  • ✓ Cleaners & External Maintenance Contractors

3. Mechanisms

Investigate the sequences and biological/physical mechanisms that lead to harm.

Analysis Points

  • ✓ Needlestick injury pathways
  • ✓ Ergonomic strain during prolonged surgery
  • ✓ Aerosol spread and ventilation efficiency

4. Evaluate Likelihood

Quantify the risk level to prioritize management efforts effectively.

Risk Calculation

  • ✓ Frequency of exposure vs. Severity of outcome
  • ✓ Current control effectiveness check
  • ✓ Prioritization of "Imminent Danger" hazards

5. Implement Solutions

The most critical step: executing the plan to mitigate or eliminate identified risks.

Action Plan

  • ✓ Engineering controls (e.g., safety-engineered sharps)
  • ✓ Administrative controls (staff training, rotations)
  • ✓ Personal Protective Equipment (PPE) mandates

6. Document Findings

Creating a legal and practical trail of assessments and actions taken.

Required Records

  • ✓ Written risk assessments for all hazards
  • ✓ COSHH assessment logs for chemicals
  • ✓ Training sign-off sheets for new protocols

7. Regular Review

Ensuring the risk management process stays dynamic and relevant over time.

Review Triggers

  • ✓ Annual standard performance audits
  • ✓ Introduction of new dental technology or staff
  • ✓ Following any accident or reported near-miss
